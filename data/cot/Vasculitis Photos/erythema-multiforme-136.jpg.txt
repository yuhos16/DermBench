Key differentials based on this: 
1. **Erythema Nodosum (EN)**: Tender nodules on shins, but typically *not* crusted/excoriated and often associated with systemic symptoms. Pruritus is minimal. 
2. **Insect Bite Hypersensitivity**: Can cause pruritic papules/nodules, but usually asymmetric and clustered. Crusting/excoriation common. 
3. **Cutaneous Small Vessel Vasculitis (CSVV)**: Presents with palpable purpura, papules, nodules. Crusting occurs if lesions ulcerate. Favors lower legs. 
4. **Papular Urticaria**: Pruritic papules/nodules, often excoriated, but usually asymmetric. 
5. **Erythema Multiforme (EM)**: Can present with acral (distal extremities) targetoid papules/plaques. Lesions may crust or erode, especially if traumatized. Pruritus/burning can occur. Knees are a common site.
