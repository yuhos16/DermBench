Based on the description, this appears consistent with **morphea**, a form of localized scleroderma. The key features of the lesion—localized hypopigmentation, hair loss within the patch, and a smoother texture—are indicative of this condition. Morphea typically presents as a well-defined, irregularly shaped area of skin that can be lighter than the surrounding skin and may exhibit a shiny appearance due to collagen deposition. The absence of hair in the affected area is also a common finding, as the condition can lead to follicular destruction.
