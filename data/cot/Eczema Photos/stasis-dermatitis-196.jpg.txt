Now, I consider differential diagnoses: 
* **Cellulitis:** Would present with acute, spreading warmth, redness, pain, and swelling. This description lacks acute inflammatory signs and emphasizes chronic changes like thickening, discoloration, and scaling. Not acute. 
* **Contact Dermatitis:** Often presents with more acute vesicles (blisters), intense itching, and sharp borders correlating with allergen exposure. The chronic features (thickening, fibrosis, hemosiderin) and distal gravity-dependent pattern don't fit well. 
* **Nummular Dermatitis:** Features coin-shaped, intensely itchy plaques, often on limbs/trunk, but not specifically gravity-dependent or showing such prominent violaceous discoloration and fibrosis. 
* **Psoriasis:** Presents with well-demarcated, thick, silvery-white scales on red plaques. The violaceous hue, yellowish-brown crusting, specific distal predominance, and fibrosis are not typical. 
* **Lichen Simplex Chronicus:** Features lichenification due to chronic scratching, but it's usually localized to accessible areas (e.g., neck, ankles, wrists) and lacks the diffuse violaceous discoloration, hemosiderin deposition, and gravity-dependent pattern seen here.
