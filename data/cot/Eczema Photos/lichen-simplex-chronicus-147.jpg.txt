Based on your description, this appears to be **lichen simplex chronicus**. It's a condition where chronic scratching or rubbing—often due to stress, habit, or an underlying itch—causes the skin to thicken, scale, and develop that characteristic cracked, "crazy-paving" look. The redness and dryness are signs of ongoing inflammation. I recommend seeing a dermatologist for confirmation; they might suggest topical steroids to reduce inflammation and break the itch-scratch cycle, along with moisturizers for the dryness. Avoiding triggers like harsh soaps or friction can help manage it. If this is a new issue, it's worth ruling out other causes like eczema or allergies.
