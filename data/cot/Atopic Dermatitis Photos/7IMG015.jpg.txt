Your skin appears to have **keratosis pilaris (KP)**. This common, harmless condition occurs when excess keratin blocks hair follicles, creating rough, bumpy patches. It’s often seen on the upper arms, back, thighs, or cheeks. KP isn’t contagious or serious, though it can be persistent. Gentle exfoliation and moisturizers (like urea or lactic acid creams) may improve texture. If itching or inflammation develops, consult a dermatologist to rule out other issues.
