Next, I consider common differentials for pustular eruptions in hair-bearing genital regions: 

1. **Folliculitis**: Highly likely. Bacterial (often *Staph aureus*) infection of hair follicles causes pustules with erythema. The central pustule and location are classic. Shaving, friction, or sweat can trigger it. 
2. **Molluscum Contagiosum**: Possible but less fitting. Typically presents as firm, dome-shaped papules with a central *umbilication* (dimple), not pus-filled heads. Surrounding redness is uncommon unless irritated. 
3. **Genital Herpes**: Unlikely. Herpes vesicles/pustules are painful, clustered, and evolve into ulcers. No mention of pain or ulceration here. 
4. **Contact Dermatitis**: Could cause redness and papules but rarely forms true pustules unless secondarily infected. 
5. **Candidiasis**: May cause pustules in moist folds but usually has satellite lesions, scaling, and intense itching – not described.

- Molluscum papules lack pustular heads; their "core" is waxy, not purulent. 
- Significant erythema isn't typical unless scratched or inflamed. 
- Pustules suggest active infection (e.g., bacterial), not viral keratinocyte proliferation.
