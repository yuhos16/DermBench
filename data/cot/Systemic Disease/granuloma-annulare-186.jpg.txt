Considering annular lesions with Koebnerization:

It is one of the most common causes of annular lesions and is well-known for exhibiting the Koebner phenomenon, often appearing along scars, cuts, or tattoos. 4. **Tinea Corporis (Ringworm):** Presents as annular lesions with a raised border, but the border is often scaly and vesicular (tiny blisters). Koebnerization is not typical for fungal infections; they spread centrifugally, not specifically along scars. Distribution along a scar would be unusual.
