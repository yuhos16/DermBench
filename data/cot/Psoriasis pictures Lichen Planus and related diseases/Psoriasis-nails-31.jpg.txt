The combination of nail matrix signs (pitting/ridges) with nail bed changes (yellowing) *and* periungual inflammation strongly indicates psoriasis, where all components of the nail unit can be affected. 

In summary, the features described—nail pitting, yellow discoloration, longitudinal ridges, and inflamed periungual skin—are characteristic of **nail psoriasis**. This occurs due to psoriasis affecting the nail matrix and bed. The presence of pitting and periungual inflammation, alongside the yellowish discoloration, aligns closely with the pathophysiology of psoriasis, confirming it as the most likely diagnosis.
