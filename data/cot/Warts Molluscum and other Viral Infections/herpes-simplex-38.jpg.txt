1. **Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV)**: 
   - Classic presentation includes grouped vesicles on an erythematous base. 
   - Lesions often cluster and may form irregular patches. 
   - Typically localized (e.g., lips, genitals), with unaffected adjacent skin. 
   - Fluid-filled vesicles align with HSV's blistering stage. 

2. **Herpes Zoster (Shingles)**: 
   - Also causes vesicles on a red base, but usually follows a dermatomal (nerve-path) distribution. 
   - The "linear" mention could suggest this, but clustered grouping is more typical of HSV. 

3. **Contact Dermatitis**: 
   - Can present with vesicles and erythema but often shows diffuse/irregular borders, not sharply localized clusters. 
   - Lacks the classic grouped-vesicle pattern. 

4. **Dyshidrotic Eczema**: 
   - Features vesicles but typically on hands/feet and symmetrically distributed. 
   - Not usually described as "linear" or sharply demarcated. 

5. **Impetigo**: 
   - Vesicles/pustules, but they rupture to form honey-colored crusts (not mentioned here). 
   - Less likely without crusting or diffuse spread.
