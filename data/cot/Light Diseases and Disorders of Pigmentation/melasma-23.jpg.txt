- **Melasma**: Typically presents as symmetric, irregular brown patches on sun-exposed areas (forehead, cheeks, upper lip). The centrofacial pattern, confluent patches, and common association with erythema (due to inflammation) align strongly. Hormonal factors often contribute.  
- **Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH)**: Usually follows trauma or inflammation (e.g., acne), but the widespread, symmetric involvement here makes PIH less likely without a clear trigger.

- **Lupus erythematosus**: Malar rash is typically sharply demarcated and lacks prominent hyperpigmentation.
