<think> First, I observe the primary location: the nail plates and subungual area (beneath the nails) of multiple fingers. This suggests a condition primarily affecting nail structures rather than surrounding skin. </think> 

Next, I note key medical features:  
1. **Discoloration**: Yellowish-brown and whitish hues. This is common in both fungal infections (onychomycosis) and psoriasis, but the yellow-brown tint often suggests keratin debris buildup typical of fungal involvement.  
2. **Nail plate changes**: Thickening, brittleness, and crumbly texture. This points to chronic damage or infection disrupting nail matrix integrity. The irregular crumbling is highly characteristic of fungal destruction.  
3. **Onycholysis**: Separation of the nail plate from the bed. While seen in psoriasis/trauma, the *debris accumulation* beneath the nails is a hallmark of fungal infection (dermatophytes digest keratin, creating subungual debris).  
4. **Longitudinal ridges**: These can occur in psoriasis but are non-specific; combined with other features, they don't override the stronger indicators.  

I then weigh differentials:  
- **Onychomycosis (fungal infection)**: Matches all features—discoloration, thickening, onycholysis with debris, and crumbling. Very common in fingernails.  
- **Psoriasis**: Often causes pitting, oil spots, or severe onycholysis, but *less commonly* produces significant subungual debris. Brittleness occurs, but "crumbly" texture is more fungal.  
- **Lichen planus or trauma**: Possible but less likely given multi-nail involvement and debris.  

The **debris accumulation under the nails** is the critical factor: it strongly favors fungal infection over psoriasis, as psoriasis typically lacks this hyperkeratotic material. No mention of skin lesions (e.g., plaques) also reduces psoriasis probability. 

Based on the clinical presentation, this appears to be **onychomycosis**, a fungal nail infection. I recommend consulting a dermatologist for confirmation via nail clipping tests and discussing antifungal treatment options.
